Reduction of Quadratic Form: Solved Problems

The transformation is performed using an orthogonal matrix N (where $N^T N = I$) whose columns are the normalized eigenvectors of matrix A. The change of variables is given by $X = NY$.

  1. Find the Symmetric Matrix (A)
  2. Find Eigenvalues ($\lambda$)
  3. Find and Normalize Eigenvectors
  4. Construct the Modal Matrix (N)
  5. Verify with $N^T A N = D$

Problem 1: Positive Definite Form

Reduce the quadratic form $Q = 3x_1^2 + 5x_2^2 + 3x_3^2 - 2x_2x_3 + 2x_3x_1 - 2x_1x_2$ to its canonical form and determine its nature.
Step 1: Write the Symmetric Matrix A
$$ A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 & 1 \\ -1 & 5 & -1 \\ 1 & -1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} $$
Step 2: Find the Eigenvalues ($\lambda$)

The characteristic equation $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$ gives the eigenvalues: $\lambda_1=2$, $\lambda_2=3$, $\lambda_3=6$.

Step 3: Find the Eigenvectors

For each eigenvalue, we solve $(A - \lambda I)X = 0$.

For $\lambda_1 = 2$: $X_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$

For $\lambda_2 = 3$: $X_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$

For $\lambda_3 = 6$: $X_3 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$

Step 4: Normalize Vectors and Form Matrix N

The orthogonal modal matrix N is formed by the normalized eigenvectors:

$$ N = \begin{pmatrix} -1/\sqrt{2} & 1/\sqrt{3} & 1/\sqrt{6} \\ 0 & 1/\sqrt{3} & -2/\sqrt{6} \\ 1/\sqrt{2} & 1/\sqrt{3} & 1/\sqrt{6} \end{pmatrix} $$
Step 5: Verify the Diagonalization with $N^T A N$

The product $N^T A N$ correctly yields the diagonal matrix D:

$$ D = N^T A N = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 6 \end{pmatrix} $$
Step 6: Final Canonical Form and Nature
$$ Q = 2y_1^2 + 3y_2^2 + 6y_3^2 $$

Nature: Positive Definite.

Problem 2: Indefinite Form (with Repeated Eigenvalue)

Reduce the quadratic form $Q = 8x_1^2 - 7x_2^2 + 8x_3^2 + 8x_1x_2 - 2x_1x_3 + 8x_2x_3$ to its canonical form and find its nature.
Step 1: Write the Symmetric Matrix A
$$ A = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 4 & -1 \\ 4 & -7 & 4 \\ -1 & 4 & 8 \end{pmatrix} $$
Step 2: Find the Eigenvalues ($\lambda$)

The characteristic equation gives the eigenvalues: $\lambda_1=9$, $\lambda_2=9$, $\lambda_3=-9$.

Step 3: Find the Eigenvectors

For $\lambda_3 = -9$:

Solving $(A+9I)X=0$ yields the eigenvector $X_3 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -4 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.

For repeated $\lambda_1 = \lambda_2 = 9$:

Solving $(A-9I)X=0$ gives the condition $-x_1 + 4x_2 - x_3 = 0$. Two orthogonal eigenvectors that satisfy this are:

$X_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $X_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -4 \\ -17 \end{pmatrix}$

Step 4: Normalize Vectors and Form Matrix N

The orthogonal matrix N is:

$$ N = \begin{pmatrix} 4/\sqrt{17} & 1/\sqrt{306} & 1/\sqrt{18} \\ 1/\sqrt{17} & -4/\sqrt{306} & -4/\sqrt{18} \\ 0 & -17/\sqrt{306} & 1/\sqrt{18} \end{pmatrix} $$
Step 5: Final Canonical Form and Nature
$$ Q = 9y_1^2 + 9y_2^2 - 9y_3^2 $$

Nature: Indefinite.
